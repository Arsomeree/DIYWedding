DIY Guide on food and drink for your wedding

Here you will find some hand picked sites I have used for planning the amount of alcohol and food to buy for my wedding that I can highly reccomend! This will help plan you day! I would consider buying when there are offers throughout the year be careful not to buy it too in advance as some drinks can expire. Also consider supermarkets as you can return any unopened drinks!


Drinks calculator!

How to calculate the quanitity of drinks required



Have you thought about evening food?

Calculating the amount of how much alachol or soft drinks to buy can be a right headache!
This handy calculator takes the stress out of worry how much to provide. Another tip is to ask you guests to bring their own alchol if they prefer a specific drink.
Don't try and provide too much choice it can prove to be difficult to calculate and cause alot of waste!


Glass rental

Waitrose and other supermarkets...




Have you thought about renting glasses for free?

IF you have a blank canvas you will need nearly everything. One thing to consider is glasses if you are organising your own bar!

Waitrose and other supermarkets like tesco, morrosions and sainbury's provide FREE glass rental!


DIY Buffet

M&S party food to order...




Want to organise your own evening food?

If you want to save some money how about organising your own eveneing buffet. Most supermarkets provide a food to order service. For example M&S, Waitrose, sainburys, morisons... etc.


Food calculator

Supermarket delivery & Oliver Platt events staff...




Want a your own bar?

If you want to provide your own food you can use this handy food caluclator and just do a tesco delivery and order some nibbles like olives, quiche, bread, cheese etc. You will need family, friends to help set this up or you can hire staff. We hired from Oliver Platt events they were extremely professional, friendly and helpful!


Cake

DIY Cake ideas...




Consider a DIY wedding cake?

My mother-in-law kindly offered to make our wedding cake. So consider asking friends or family to help or do it yourself. It doesn't have to be a cake! It could be mini cupcakes, doughnut tower, oreo tower etc. Use your imagination :) You can even get cheaper wedding cakes from food to order supermarkets like M&S.


sweet Tables

DIY sweet tables...




Have you considered building your own sweet table?

If you want a cool and quirky DIY sweet table you can build your own sweet cart! I went to home bargain and charity shop to get cheap sweet jars. You can also get lolly tree/ marshmellow DIY tree sets from home bargain which go down well.I have included a pinterest guide to some oinspirational DIY sweet tables. Try and buy sweets on offer throughtout the year. Best time to buy is just after easter and in the new year sales!

Center pieces

DIY Table decor...

Inject your own style into your special day! DIY your tables using pintrest you can get some inspiration. I went to charity shops and collect old books to build my table centres. On top of the stacked books I used old science glassware with flowers in them. I had surplus confetti so scattered it around the books and little log candles darted around the books.
